School Policy School Year 2022-2023

The following policies are highlighted and are anchored from the DepEd order no. 40, series of 2012. These are the most common tools that authorities should focus on the implementation of school policy for school year 2022-2023 considering the current situation we are all experiencing in the new normal modality of learning.

Some of our learners in these trying times are exposed and vulnerable to such challenges. The school committee on school policy and child protection program is adhering to the call of the department in protecting the welfare of these learners.

Every child deserves protection and should be given equal rights, the right to live, to be educated, to be protected, for their development and improvement.

1. Attendance

Students are required to attend his/her classes every day from Monday to Friday. In case of his/her absence, he/she should secure a letter of excuse duly signed by his/her parent/guardian. If the student is absent for 3 or more days consecutively, the class adviser will make a follow-up through call or text, sending a letter and if there is no reply, the class adviser and the school guidance counselor will do a home visitation.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers should follow-up with the learners through calls, text, chat, or through their Group Chats (GCs).

2. In the event, the learner has not complied with the call of the adviser, the Guidance Coordinator will intervene and invite the parents/guardians through sending letters for a short conference.

2. Tardiness

Coming to school late for 30 minutes during the first-period class and or on the succeeding classes (8 academic subjects) is considered an act of tardiness. Similar to attendance, students who failed to comply with the needed tasks and modules (blended learning) on time are considered late. Late submission of requirements and other related tasks should be noted by the adviser for future reference and guidance.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers should follow-up with learners through calls, text, chat, or through their Group Chats (GCs).

2. In the event, the learner has not complied with the call of the adviser, the class adviser will refer the case to the school disciplinarian. The Guidance Coordinator will intervene and invite the parents/guardians for a short conference as needed.

3. Cutting Classes

This is a very common issue in all schools especially during face-to-face classes; students who are not attending classes regularly are considered as cutting class.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers should follow-up with the learners through calls, text, chat, or through their Group Chats (GCs).

2. In the event, the learner has not complied with the call of the adviser for three (3) times, the class adviser will refer the case to the school disciplinarian. The Guidance Coordinator will intervene and invite the parents/guardians for a short conference as needed.

4. Physical Abuse

Refers to the maltreatment of a child, whether habitual or not, which includes any of the following: psychological or physical abuse, neglect, cruelty, sexual abuse, and emotional maltreatment.

No one deserves abuse in any form. Learners in this time of pandemic (New Normal) experience physical abuse within the premises of their houses and even outside their respective homes. This form of abuse also occurs in peer organizations in the community (gang and other related notorious groups) wherein parents should strictly monitor the whereabouts of their children. Learners also experience mental depression where the behavior affects at some point. In this connection, parents and guardians should be the first to understand and embrace the needs of their children. Teachers, especially the advisers, should always make a follow-up in monitoring the condition of their students.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers should call the attention of the offending student/s through calls, text, or chat and refer the case to the school disciplinarian.

2. The school disciplinarian or the Grade Level Coordinator comes in to deliberate the case and reprimand the offending party.

3. The Guidance Coordinator will intervene upon referral from the disciplinarian/Grade Level Coordinator and will invite the parents/guardians through phone calls or text messages for proper coordination and utmost response, if not solved at the school level.

5. Verbal Abuse

This is a very strong form of abuse that is happening around. Learners of this generation, with the present condition of the learning modality, tend to overdo and overreact to mild issues, becoming very brutal in terms of expressing themselves with offensive words.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers should call the attention of the learners through calls, text, or chat and refer the case to the school disciplinarian.

2. The school disciplinarian or the Grade Level Coordinator comes in to deliberate the case and incident.

3. The Guidance Coordinator will intervene upon referral and invite the parents/guardians for proper coordination and immediate action if necessary.

6. Bullying/Cyber Bullying

Bullying refers to willful aggressive behavior directed towards a particular victim who may be outnumbered, younger, weak, with a disability, less confident, or otherwise vulnerable. Cyberbullying is any conduct resulting in harassment, intimidation through electronic means or other technology (e.g., texting, email, instant messaging, social media).

This act is expressed by many people, especially teenagers of this generation. They seem to remain calm and think of it as a normal thing, but bullying really causes a lot of depression and leaves a mark on the person's lives. Cyberbullying has become a perennial problem with social media subscribers. Students engaging in cyberbullying and other forms, such as sharing indecent photos or malicious videos on social media, are prohibited. Any hurtful words or messages sent or shared directly or indirectly on any platforms should be addressed.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers should call the attention of the offending student/s through calls, text, or chat and refer the case to the school disciplinarian.

2. The school disciplinarian or the Grade Level Coordinator comes in to deliberate the case and reprimand the offending party.

3. The Guidance Coordinator will intervene upon referral from the disciplinarian/Grade Level Coordinator and will invite the parents/guardians through phone calls or text messages for proper coordination and utmost response, if not solved at the school level.

7. Child Exploitation (Labor/Sex)

Teenagers under 18 years of age should be dealt with careful attention. Many children are experiencing abuse in labor. Parents must take full responsibility in adhering to these concerns to the school authorities and even in the barangay office for proper guidance and protection.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers should call the attention of the parents/guardians through calls, text, or chat and refer the case to the school disciplinarian.

2. The school disciplinarian comes in to deliberate the case and incident that the child has experienced.

3. The Guidance Coordinator will intervene upon referral from the disciplinarian and will invite the parents/guardians through phone calls or text messages for proper coordination and utmost response.

4. The Barangay Council for the Protection of Children (BCPC) should intervene if the conference between the parents and children did not meet the agreement. The BCPC will strictly monitor the welfare of these learners with the support of the school authorities.

8. Loitering

Students in the Junior and Senior High must observe proper protocols in public areas and should not display any indecent act, especially inside the school premises. Parents and guardians must monitor their children's whereabouts, especially after class in the afternoon.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers should call the attention of the learners through calls, text, chat, or through their Group Chats (GCs).

2. In the event, the learner has not complied with the call of the adviser, the Guidance Coordinator will intervene and invite the parents/guardians through phone calls or text messages for a short conference.

9. Smoking/Vaping (Electronic Cigarette) Inside the Campus and Within the School Vicinity

Students and school personnel are NOT allowed to smoke or vape inside the school campus or within the school vicinity. If caught, parents and guardians will be informed regarding the incident.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers should call the attention of the learners through calls, text, chat, or through their Group Chats (GCs).

2. In the event, the learner has not complied with the call of the adviser, the Guidance Coordinator will intervene and invite the parents/guardians through phone calls or text messages for a short conference.

10. Carry or Transport Firearms or Deadly Weapons

Students and school personnel are NOT allowed to carry or transport firearms or deadly weapons inside the school campus. If caught, parents and guardians will be informed, and the case will be referred to proper authorities.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers, school disciplinarian, and guardian coordinator should call the attention of the learner(s) and parent or guardians through calls, text, chat, or through their Group Chats (GCS).

2. The case should be referred to the proper authority like barangay officials and/or Philippine National Police.

11. Carrying and Using of Illegal Drugs/Prohibited Drugs

Students, teaching, and non-teaching personnel are prohibited from bringing illegal drugs inside the school campus or engaging in illegal trading with such substances.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers, school disciplinarian, and guardian coordinator should call the attention of the learner(s) and parent or guardians through calls, text, chat, or through their Group Chats (GCs).

2. The case should be referred to the proper authority like barangay officials and/or Philippine National Police.

12. Wearing of School Uniforms and Proper Grooming

Students must adhere to the policy of wearing the proper school uniform at all times inside the school premises. Male students must follow proper haircut guidelines for discipline and uniformity in grooming.

Action Taken:

1. Advisers should call the attention of the learners through calls, text, chat, or through their Group Chats (GCs).

2. In the event, the learner has not complied with the call of the adviser, the Guidance Coordinator will intervene and invite the parents/guardians for a short conference.

Duties and Responsibilities of Pupils, Students/ Learners

Pupils, students/learners shall have the following duties and responsibilities:

A. Comply with the school's regulations, as long as they are in harmony with their best interests. Pupils, students/ learners shall strictly follow the following

I. Engaging in discrimination, or leading a group of students to discriminate another, with reference to one's physical appearance, weaknesses and status of any sort;

II. Doing any act that is inappropriate or sexually provocative;

III. Participating in behavior of other students that is illegal, unsafe or abusive;

IV.Marking or damaging school property, including books, any way;

V.Engaging in fights or any aggressive behavior;

VI.Wearing of proper uniform and dress is highly encouraged;

VII. Observing proper haircut for the males;

VIII. Introducing into the school premises or otherwise possessing prohibited articles, such as deadly weapons, drugs, alcohol, toxic and noxious substances, cigarettes and pornographic materials

XI. Performing other similar acts that cause damage or injury to another.
